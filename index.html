  • 1986 Argentina 3–2 West Germany: At Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, Diego Maradona led Argentina to a 3-2 victory over West Germany, producing some of the most memorable moments in World Cup history.

  • 1978 Argentina 3–1 (a.e.t.) Netherlands: Estadio Monumental in Buenos Aires witnessed Argentina securing their first World Cup title, defeating the Netherlands 3-1 after extra time.

  • 1974 West Germany 2–1 Netherlands: The final at Olympiastadion in Munich, West Germany, saw the hosts triumph over the Netherlands 2-1, with Franz Beckenbauer leading his team to victory.

  • 1970 Brazil 4–1 Italy: Estadio Azteca in Mexico City witnessed a mesmerizing final as Brazil defeated Italy 4-1. This match showcased the flair and brilliance of Brazilian football.

  • 1966 England 4–2 (a.e.t.) West Germany: The iconic Wembley Stadium in London hosted a dramatic final, where England triumphed in extra time against West Germany, winning 4-2 and claiming their first and only World Cup victory.

  • 1962 Brazil 3–1 Czechoslovakia: At Estadio Nacional in Santiago, Chile, Brazil clinched their second consecutive title by beating Czechoslovakia 3-1, showcasing their dominance in world football.

  • 1958 Brazil 5–2 Sweden: Råsunda Stadium in Solna, Sweden, witnessed Brazil's emergence as a football superpower. They defeated Sweden 5-2, with a young Pelé making a significant impact on the international stage.

  • 1954 West Germany 3–2 Hungary: West Germany emerged victorious at Wankdorf Stadium in Bern, Switzerland, defeating Hungary 3-2 in a closely contested match that became famously known as the "Miracle of Bern."

  • 1950 Uruguay 2–1 Brazil: The Maracanã Stadium in Rio de Janeiro hosted a historic final between neighbors Uruguay and Brazil. Uruguay shocked the hosts by winning 2-1 in front of an astonishing crowd of 173,850, known as the "Maracanazo."

  • 1938Italy 4–2 Hungary: The final in Stade Olympique de Colombes, Paris, saw Italy once again clinching the title, this time by defeating Hungary 4-2, and solidifying their status as a football powerhouse.

  • 1934 Italy 2–1 (a.e.t.) Czechoslovakia: Stadio Nazionale PNF in Rome hosted the final, where Italy triumphed in extra time against Czechoslovakia, securing a 2-1 victory. This was Italy's first World Cup win.

  • 1930 Uruguay 4–2 Argentina: The inaugural World Cup final took place at Estadio Centenario in Montevideo, Uruguay. Uruguay emerged victorious, defeating Argentina 4-2, in a match that marked the birth of the prestigious tournament.
